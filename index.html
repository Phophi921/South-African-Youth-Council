About SAYC

The South African Youth Council (SAYC) emerged during a pivotal time in South Africa's history, marking a significant milestone in the country's journey towards democratic youth representation. The official establishment of SAYC on August 20, 1997, came just three years after South Africa's first democratic elections in 1994, symbolizing the dawn of a new era for youth participation in national development. The formation of SAYC was not an isolated event but the culmination of years of struggle and advocacy for youth rights and representation. Its roots can be traced back to the youth movements of the apartheid era, where young people played a crucial role in the fight for freedom and equality. The transition to democracy created a unique opportunity to institutionalize youth participation in governance and development processes.

ESTABLISHMENT TIMELINE

June 16, 1976: The Soweto Uprising marks a turning point in youth activism in South Africa. This event, where students protested against the imposition of Afrikaans as a medium of instruction, highlights the power of youth in driving social change.

1980s Youth organizations play a crucial role in the anti-apartheid struggle, laying the groundwork for future youth representation.

April 27, 1994: South Africa holds its first democratic elections. This monumental event sets the stage for new youth development structures

June 16, 1996: The National Youth Commission Act is passed, creating a framework for youth participation in governance

August 20, 1997: The South African Youth Council (SAYC) is officially launched, marking a new era in organized youth representation.

1998-1999: Provincial branches of SAYC, including KwaZulu-Natal, are established. This period sees intense activity as youth leaders work to set up structures and programs tailored to their regions.

June 16, 2000: The first National Youth Day is celebrated under the new democracy, with SAYC playing a key role in organizing events and discussions.

2008: A major review and restructuring of SAYC takes place, aiming to make the organization more effective and responsive to youth needs.

2015: SAYC aligns its strategies with the National Youth Policy 2015-2020, focusing on education, economic participation, and health and well-being.

June 16, 2020: Amidst the global COVID-19 pandemic, SAYC leads virtual Youth Day celebrations, showcasing adaptability in challenging times.

2020-Present: SAYC continues to evolve, now working within the framework of the National Youth Policy 2020-2030, with a renewed focus on digital skills, entrepreneurship, and climate action.

Vision & Mission

Vision

To create a unified, representative platform for South African youth to actively participate in shaping the country's future, fostering a society where every young person is empowered, engaged, and equipped to reach their full potential.

Mission

To coordinate, lobby, and advocate for youth development in South Africa by uniting youth organizations behind a common vision of holistic youth empowerment and acting as the voice of the youth in matters of national importance."

Founding Principles

  • Youth Empowerment: Recognizing young people as agents of change and key stakeholders in national development.
  • Democratic Representation: Ensuring diverse youth voices are heard in decision-making processes.
  • Non-Partisanship: Maintaining independence from political parties while engaging with all stakeholders.
  • Inclusivity: Embracing diversity and promoting equal opportunities for all youth.
  • Accountability: Upholding transparency and accountability to youth constituencies.
