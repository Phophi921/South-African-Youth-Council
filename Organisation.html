Organizational Structure & Affiliations

This section describes SAYC’s structure and the types of organizations affiliated with it, showing how decisions flow from national to local levels.

Organizational Structure

National Executive Committee (NEC)

Highest decision-making body; provides overall strategic direction with elected youth leaders from across the country.

Provincial Executive Committees (PEC)

Comprised of 15 elected leaders per province, overseeing provincial youth initiatives and advocacy.

Regional Executive Committees (REC)

Represent youth in districts; 10 elected leaders ensure programs address local needs.

Branch Executive Committees (BEC)

Grassroots level; 10 leaders per branch working at local municipalities linking SAYC with communities.

Types of Organizations Affiliated to SAYC

Service-Based Organizations

Provide direct support and services to youth, including youth-owned entities, NPOs, private Pty Ltd companies, and others. Align with constitutional rights to education, health care, and social security, fostering economic empowerment.

Issue-Based Organizations

Operate under rights to freedom of expression and assembly, providing platforms for youth engagement and social change. Includes NGOs, NPOs, and CBOs advocating for youth-related issues.

Membership-Based Organizations

Enable individuals to participate in collective decision-making, such as political youth formations and student representative bodies, grounded in the right to freedom of association and democratic participation.
